User Guide

Reports / Financial Intelligence & Partner Distribution

Maly - OBS (Online Business System) · Version 1.0

Module Purpose

The Reports module provides the financial overview of your business: assets, liabilities, KPIs, partner distribution, and scenario simulation. You see Net Profit, Revenue, Net Equity, Liquidity, ROI, Burn Rate, Runway, and a detailed breakdown of assets and liabilities. A simulation engine lets you adjust delivery rate, pipeline logic (ship all vs ready only), and inventory valuation to explore "what-if" scenarios. Partners are managed with share percentages, capital, injections, withdrawals, and profit distribution. You can export reports to PDF, close financial periods, and execute partner exits with strict payout calculations.

Step-by-Step Workflow

1. View Financial Breakdown

  • Open Financial Breakdown tab (default).
  • View the 8 KPI cards.
  • Click any KPI card to open the Breakdown dialog with the formula.
  • Scroll to see Assets and Liabilities tables.
  • Toggle Exclude (Liquid Mode) on Fixed Assets to see liquid equity only.
  • View Monthly Performance charts and table; click column headers to sort.

2. Run a Simulation

  • Adjust Delivery Rate slider (0–100%).
  • Adjust Inventory Valuation Rate slider (0–100%).
  • Set Pipeline Execution: Ship All Pipeline or Ship Ready Only.
  • Optionally click Settings to set return cost, moderator bonus, packaging cost.
  • Click Run Simulation.
  • KPIs, assets, liabilities, and Partners table update with simulated values.

3. Reset Simulation

  • Click Reset.
  • Simulation is cleared; values return to book (historical).

4. Add a Partner

  • Open Partners & Distribution tab.
  • Click Add Partner.
  • Enter Name (required).
  • Enter Share % (required; must be > 0; total shares cannot exceed 100%).
  • Enter Capital (optional; initial investment).
  • Click Save. Success: green snackbar; table refreshes.

5. Edit a Partner

  • In Partners table, click Edit (✎) on the row.
  • Change Name or Share %.
  • Capital cannot be changed here.
  • Click Update. Success: green snackbar; table refreshes.

6. Add a Partner Transaction

  • In Partners table, click Add Transaction (💳) on the row.
  • Select Type: Withdrawal, Cash Injection, or Capital Increase.
  • Enter Amount (required).
  • Optionally add Notes. Click Save.
  • Success: green snackbar. Withdrawal requires sufficient treasury.

7. View Partner History

  • Click the partner Name in the Partners table.
  • A dialog shows Injections, Withdrawals, and Profit Share transactions.

8. Exit a Partner

  • In Partners table, click Delete (🗑) on the row.
  • Pre-validation: "Cannot exit the last remaining partner" or "Total shares must equal exactly 100% before executing an exit" if not met.
  • Partner Exit dialog opens with valuation controls, Recalculate, and breakdown.
  • Choose Pay Now (from Treasury) or create a Payable (due date required).
  • Click Finalize Exit & Remove Partner. Success: partner removed; share redistributed.

9. Close a Financial Period

  • Use the Close Period action (if available in the UI).
  • Validates that total shares = 100%.
  • Archives the period; moves partner dues to Debts; clears partners ledger.
  • "Cannot Close: Total Shares are X%. Must be 100%." if shares ≠ 100%.

10. Export to PDF

  • Click Export PDF in the header.
  • A PDF report is generated and opens.
  • Report includes current KPIs, simulation params (if active), partners, assets/liabilities.
  • Saved to a fixed path (e.g., Desktop/Reports/Financial report). Success: "PDF saved to [path]" (green).

Tips

  • Click KPI cards to see the exact formula and components (Explainable BI).
  • Simulation is indicative; delivery rate affects revenue; return/mod bonus/packaging use order counts from settings.
  • Distribution Base = what can be distributed to partners (after effective capital).
  • Total Shares bar: green when 100%; red when not — fix before closing or exiting.
  • Partner Exit uses book value for payout; simulation does not affect exit math.
  • Exclude Fixed Assets shows liquid equity only (no fixed assets in assets total).
  • Monthly Performance shows last 12 months; sort by clicking column headers.
  • Export PDF uses current view (simulated or book) and includes report parameters.
  • Capital in Edit Partner is read-only; use Capital Increase transaction to add.
  • Company Treasury can hold shares when a partner exits (shares transferred to keep total at 100%).
