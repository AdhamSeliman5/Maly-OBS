User Guide

Shipping

Maly - OBS (Online Business System) · Version 1.0

Module Purpose

The Shipping module manages orders that have been handed over to a courier (shipping company). You select a company, view its active (in-transit) orders, update their delivery status (Delivered, Partial, Returned, Damaged), adjust shipping fees and extra costs, then process collection to finalize them. The module also tracks loans (advances) between you and the courier, generates collection reports, and exports PDF analytics. Net settlement uses total amount minus shipping fees (deposit is not used in the calculation).

Step-by-Step Workflow

1. Select a Shipping Company

  • Open the Shipping module.
  • Use the Shipping Company dropdown to choose a courier.
  • The system loads active orders, ledger, and analytics for that company.

2. Add or Delete a Shipping Company

Add

  • Click the green + icon.
  • Enter the company name.
  • Click Add.
  • Success: "Success" snackbar.

Delete

  • Select the company in the dropdown.
  • Click the red trash icon.
  • Confirm in the dialog.
  • Success: "Deleted". If orders are still assigned: "Cannot delete: X orders still assigned".

3. Search Orders

  • Type in the Search field (order ID, customer name, phone, governorate).
  • Press Enter.
  • The orders list filters and pagination resets.

4. Update Order Status and Amounts

  • In the Active Orders tab, for each order: Ship Cost (edit fees; Net recalculates), Status (Delivered, Partial, Returned, Damaged), Extra (when Returned/Damaged — Shipped on Sender cost or compensation), Date (pick delivery date).
  • Check the orders you want to settle.
  • For Partial status: when you check the order, a Partial Delivery dialog opens. Enter delivered quantities and unit prices per item, then Confirm. New price = sum(delivered × unit price) + shipping.

5. Process Collection

  • Check the orders to settle.
  • Click Process Collection (X).
  • In the dialog: selected count, Total Delivered, Shipping, Extra, Net Amount, Unified Delivery Date (optional), Cash Received (default = Net), Report Orders Older Than (Days) (default 7).
  • Click Process.
  • The system: updates order statuses, records ledger entries, generates Collection Excel report (Settlement Details, Pending Orders, Stale Orders), opens the file.
  • Success: "Processed Successfully!" and "Collection report saved and opened".

6. Add a Loan

  • Go to the Ledger tab.
  • Click Add Loan.
  • Positive: company receives advance from courier (Treasury increases). Negative: company gives advance to courier (Treasury decreases; must have sufficient treasury).
  • Click Add.
  • Success: "Loan Added". If insufficient: "Insufficient treasury balance. (Current: X)".

7. Repay a Loan

  • In the Ledger tab, find an unpaid loan (has Repay button).
  • Click Repay.
  • Enter the Repay Amount (default = original amount).
  • Click Repay.
  • Success: "Repaid Successfully".

8. Export PDF Report

  • Select a shipping company.
  • Click the PDF icon in the header.
  • Loading overlay: "Generating PDF...".
  • The system creates a PDF in Desktop\Reports\Shipping\ and opens it.
  • Success: "PDF Report Saved: [path]".

Tips

  • Unified Delivery Date: Use when all selected orders were delivered on the same date.
  • Partial Delivery: For partial shipments, open the dialog, enter delivered quantities and unit prices per item.
  • Returned + Extra: When a return has "Shipped on Sender" cost, set status to Returned and fill Extra. The system records it as Shipped on Sender.
  • Damaged + Extra: Enter compensation amount in Extra for damaged items.
  • Net Due = Pending − Loan; negative means you owe the courier.
  • Collection Excel: Includes Settlement Details, Pending Orders, and Stale Orders (orders older than the entered days).
  • Ledger: Loan (سلفة), Order Closing, Cash Payout, Loan Repayment, Return Cost, Compensation appear as different transaction types.
